A new analysis from the Municipal Research and Services Center (MRSC) of Washington documented a growing pattern: with the state legislature unable to pass a statewide kratom framework, cities are writing their own rules. At least four Washington municipalities have enacted outright kratom bans since March, and the MRSC reported that more are considering similar action.

## Washington cities step into the regulatory gap

The Washington State Legislature considered but did not pass HB 2291, the Kratom Consumer Protection Act (KCPA), during its 2026 session. The bill would have restricted kratom sales to adults 21 and older, regulated production and retail in a manner similar to cannabis, and included a state preemption clause that would have prevented local bans.

Without that preemption, cities have moved on their own. [The MRSC cataloged four municipalities](https://mrsc.org/stay-informed/mrsc-insight/may-2026/kratom-bans) that have enacted kratom bans:

- **Spokane** passed Ordinance C36820, effective April 8, banning all kratom sales. Violations are civil infractions; possession is not prohibited. [The Spokesman-Review reported](https://www.spokesman.com/stories/2026/mar/02/spokane-bans-sale-of-kratom/) that the council voted unanimously.
- **Spokane Valley** enacted a similar ban effective March 16.
- **Cle Elum** passed a ban covering kratom products within city limits.
- **Othello** passed Ordinance 1647, effective May 1, banning the sale, advertisement, and distribution of kratom and 7-OH products. [The Columbia Basin Herald reported](https://columbiabasinherald.com/news/2026/apr/14/a-real-concern-for-our-youth/) that council members cited youth access as a primary concern.

The MRSC analysis noted that the absence of state preemption leaves the door open for additional municipalities to act. Washington has no state law prohibiting or regulating kratom under any agency's authority, creating what the analysis described as a regulatory vacuum that local governments are filling city by city.

The pattern mirrors a broader national trend. As states debate between consumer protection frameworks and outright bans, the regulatory patchwork grows more complex for manufacturers and retailers operating across multiple jurisdictions.

## Utah appeal moves forward

In a related development, [the Deseret News reported](https://www.deseret.com/lifestyle/2026/05/11/challenge-to-kratom-law-shut-down-appeal/) that the Global Kratom Coalition and Botanic Tonics LLC formally filed their appeal with the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Tenth Circuit after [a federal judge denied their request to block Utah's Kratom Regulation Act last week][utah-kratom-act-takes-effect-manufacturers-reformulate]. The law remains in effect during the appeal. The case will test whether states can restrict kratom products that comply with federal standards, a question with implications for the growing number of state-level frameworks.
