## TL;DR

Missouri Attorney General Catherine Hanaway announced a statewide investigation into kratom manufacturers and retailers, issuing six Civil Investigative Demands to companies including CBD Kratom and CBD American Shaman over alleged failures to disclose 7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH) content. Connecticut became the seventh state to schedule kratom as a controlled substance, and Tennessee's legislature passed a full ban on kratom sales and possession.

## Missouri AG Issues Six Civil Investigative Demands

Missouri Attorney General Catherine Hanaway [announced](https://ago.mo.gov/attorney-general-hanaway-launches-investigation-into-major-kratom-manufacturers-and-retailers-for-potentially-dangerous-and-illegal-practices/) a statewide investigation into kratom manufacturers and distributors, saying consumers may be exposed to "unapproved, unsafe, and deceptively marketed opioid-like drugs." The office issued six Civil Investigative Demands (CIDs) to CBD Kratom, The Green Dragon CBD, Emporium Smoke Shop, Moonlight Smoke Shop, CBD American Shaman, and Shaman Botanicals.

The CIDs seek information on possible failures to disclose psychoactive substances, with a particular focus on 7-OH, according to the attorney general's office. The investigation is the second major state-level AG action in a week, following Alabama AG Steve Marshall's [statewide cease-and-desist order][kansas-signs-7-oh-ban-washington-weighs-kratom-tax] targeting businesses that sell products containing mitragynine or 7-OH.

Alabama's order, issued last week, said lab testing confirmed kratom alkaloids in products on store shelves and that those substances are illegal to possess, sell, or offer for sale under Alabama law, [according to the AG's office](https://www.alabamaag.gov/alabama-attorney-general-issues-statewide-cease-and-desist-order-targeting-illegal-kratom-products/). Marshall's office said it is coordinating enforcement with local district attorneys and law enforcement agencies.

## Connecticut Becomes Seventh State to Schedule Kratom

Connecticut's Department of Consumer Protection [confirmed](https://portal.ct.gov/dcp/knowledge-base/articles/drug-control/patients/kratom-update-october-2025) that kratom and 7-OH have been added to the state's controlled-substance schedule, making Connecticut the seventh state to classify kratom as a Schedule I substance. The scheduling took effect after final regulatory approval on April 2, 2026, according to the department's notice.

The move places Connecticut alongside Alabama, Arkansas, Indiana, Rhode Island, Vermont, and Wisconsin as states that have fully scheduled kratom. The action is distinct from the regulatory frameworks adopted in states like Utah and Nevada, which impose age limits and manufacturing standards rather than outright prohibition.

## Tennessee Legislature Passes Full Kratom Ban

The Tennessee legislature passed a full ban on kratom after weeks of debate over the substance's effects, [according to ABC News 4](https://abcnews4.com/news/nation-world/gas-station-heroin-tn-passes-full-kratom-ban-after-weeks-long-debate-over-effects). The vote adds Tennessee to a growing list of states choosing prohibition over the consumer-protection model that groups like the American Kratom Association have advocated for in state legislatures.

The Tennessee vote followed a period of intensifying state action that has included [bipartisan Senate pressure on the FDA][senators-push-full-kratom-scheduling-states-crack-down] to schedule all kratom, Kansas's signing of a [7-OH scheduling bill][kansas-signs-7-oh-ban-washington-weighs-kratom-tax], and [New York's adoption of age limits and warning labels][new-york-signs-kratom-age-limit-michigan-house-passes-ban].

## New York Introduces Full Scheduling Bill

Separately from the age-limit and labeling laws that [Gov. Kathy Hochul signed last week][new-york-signs-kratom-age-limit-michigan-house-passes-ban], a New York State Senate bill, [S7379](https://www.nysenate.gov/legislation/bills/2025/S7379), would place kratom on the state's Schedule I controlled-substances list. The bill's stated purpose is to "prevent the sale and use of kratom," according to the legislative text. The bill's prospects in the current session are unclear; the Senate has not scheduled committee action.

The dual-track approach in New York, where regulation and prohibition are advancing simultaneously across different legislative bodies, reflects a pattern visible in several states this month.
