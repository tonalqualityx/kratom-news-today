The Revere Board of Health held a public hearing on June 18 on a proposed ordinance that would ban kratom sales citywide, according to the [Advocate News](https://advocatenews.net/revere/news/revere-board-of-health-weighs-ban-on-kratom-sales/). A formal vote is scheduled for June 26. The hearing drew 15 written comments, 14 of which opposed the ban, though no Revere residents spoke during the public comment period.

Board Chair Dr. Drew Bunker cited a family friend's kratom overdose death and concerns about product potency as reasons to move forward. "We need to act. Lives are at stake," Bunker said, according to the Advocate News. Board members Kathleen Savage and Viviana Catano agreed that the risks posed by synthetic kratom products justified local action.

Allison Smith, director of the Global Kratom Coalition, testified in opposition. Smith acknowledged issues with synthetic kratom products but argued that thousands of studies show adverse effects from natural kratom are rare, and that roughly 23 million Americans use kratom safely, the Advocate News reported.

Claire Inzerillo, a policy writer in the City Solicitor's office, presented three options for the board: wait for action on three pending bills in the Massachusetts state legislature, implement a blanket sales ban, or ban synthetic kratom products while permitting natural leaf kratom. Inzerillo noted that the third option would present enforcement challenges because product packaging does not always distinguish between natural and synthetic formulations, according to the report.

Lauren Buck, Revere's director of public health, outlined the health concerns driving the proposal. The hearing referenced data points including 91 kratom-related deaths tracked by the CDC and a 2017 DEA public health advisory identifying kratom as a drug of concern.

Revere is the latest Massachusetts municipality to weigh local kratom restrictions as statewide legislation remains stalled. Rockland's Board of Health [enacted an immediate ban in May][white-house-drug-strategy-kratom-massachusetts-local-bans], and Hanover voted on a similar measure in June. Three bills addressing kratom regulation are currently pending in the Massachusetts Legislature, though none have advanced out of committee. The pattern of local boards of health acting independently while awaiting state action mirrors dynamics playing out in other states where legislatures have been slow to address kratom at the state level.

The June 26 vote will determine whether Revere joins the growing list of Massachusetts municipalities that have restricted kratom sales through local health authority.
