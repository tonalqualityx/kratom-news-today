# Research Configuration

## Topic

Kratom News Today covers the kratom industry across three beats: regulation, business, and science. The publication monitors developments affecting kratom — Mitragyna speciosa and its alkaloids — including federal regulatory action, state-level legislation, court decisions, enforcement activity, business news affecting manufacturers and retailers, scientific research, and harm-reduction discourse.

Particular areas of attention:

- Federal regulatory posture (FDA scheduling considerations, DEA actions, FTC enforcement, Customs and Border Protection import activity)
- State-level legislation, regulation, and enforcement (the kratom regulatory patchwork is the most active in the industry)
- Court decisions involving kratom companies, retailers, or consumers
- Business developments: company news, M&A activity, lawsuits, retail and wholesale trends, product category developments
- Scientific research: peer-reviewed studies on kratom and its alkaloids (mitragynine, 7-hydroxymitragynine, and related compounds), NIH-funded research, university programs, harm-reduction studies
- 7-OH (7-hydroxymitragynine) specifically — this compound is a current regulatory focus, drives much of the news cycle, and matters commercially for the industry
- Payment processing and platform risk for the industry (BRAM enforcement, banking access, ad platform decisions)

## Geographic Scope

Primarily United States — federal and state action. Brief coverage of significant international developments (Thailand, EU, UK regulatory positions) when they affect US discourse or industry strategy. Not exhaustive on global coverage.

## Time Window

Daily briefings use a 24-hour research window. May be extended to 48-72 hours when news cycles are slow, or shortened when significant breaking news is in play.

## Source Preferences

**Strongly preferred:**
- Primary regulatory documents (FDA notices, court filings, state legislation text, agency rulings)
- Peer-reviewed scientific journals (Frontiers in Pharmacology, Drug and Alcohol Dependence, Addiction, Clinical Pharmacology & Therapeutics, and related)
- Established trade press (Marijuana Moment, MJBizDaily when they cover kratom-adjacent issues, NutraIngredients)
- Major mainstream press doing original reporting (Bloomberg, AP, Reuters, STAT News, The Washington Post, The New York Times, Politico)
- State legislative tracking services (LegiScan and similar)
- NIH and university press releases on research

**Moderately preferred:**
- Industry advocacy organization announcements (when they contain news, not just position statements)
- Local press covering state-level kratom developments
- Industry trade publications with reporting (not just press releases)

**Acceptable but lower priority:**
- Reuters and AP pickups
- Aggregators of regulatory news

## Source Exclusions

**Never use as sources:**
- Vendor blog content masquerading as news (most company blogs in this category)
- AI-generated content farms
- Forums and social media as primary sources for factual claims (may be referenced for sentiment when attributed clearly)
- Aggregator sites with no original reporting
- Sponsored content presented as news

**Use with caution and attribution:**
- Advocacy organization content (always frame as the organization's position, not as neutral fact)
- Single-source claims that haven't been corroborated elsewhere
- Anonymous-source reporting (cite the publishing outlet's anonymous sources, never invent attribution)

## Source Count Target

8 sources per daily briefing, typical range 6-12. Sufficient to cover the day's news without padding. If a slow news day yields fewer than 6 quality sources, the briefing should be correspondingly short rather than artificially expanded.

## Notes

The publication is BRAM-aware. The research stage should focus on what's being reported, not on building narratives that overstate or sensationalize. The synthesis stage handles voice and framing; research's job is to gather attributable findings with rigorous source quality.

The publication never reports on what consumers should do, never recommends products, and never gives medical or legal advice. The research stage should be aware that findings about consumer safety, legal status, or efficacy will be reported with attribution but never as the publication's own claims.

When 7-OH research or regulatory action is in play, prioritize primary sources and peer-reviewed work over commentary. This is the most regulatorily-loaded compound in the category and accuracy matters more than usual.
